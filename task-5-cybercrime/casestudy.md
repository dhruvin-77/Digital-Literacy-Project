## UPI / Online Payment Fraud Case Study

### What is the Crime?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking information such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or login details. These frauds often involve impersonation, fake offers, or misleading payment requests.

---

### How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. The victim receives a call, message, or WhatsApp text from a fraudster pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support representative.

2. The fraudster creates a sense of urgency by claiming there is an issue with the victim’s account, a pending refund, or an attractive cashback/reward offer.

3. The victim is asked to click on a link, download an app, or scan a QR code.

4. The fraudster then sends a “collect request” or guides the victim to enter their UPI PIN under the pretext of receiving money.

5. Once the victim enters the PIN or approves the request, money is instantly deducted from their bank account.

---

### Who is Usually Targeted?

UPI frauds commonly target students, elderly individuals, and people who are new to digital payment systems. Individuals who are less aware of online safety practices are more vulnerable to such scams.

---

### Consequences of the Crime

The immediate consequence is financial loss, which can sometimes be significant. Victims may also experience stress and anxiety. In some cases, personal and banking information may be misused for further fraudulent activities. Recovery of lost money is often difficult if not reported quickly.

---

### Realistic Fictional Scenario

A college student receives a message claiming they have won a cashback reward. The message includes a QR code to “claim” the reward. Believing it to be genuine, the student scans the code and enters their UPI PIN. Instead of receiving money, an amount is deducted from their account. Later, the student realizes it was a fraud and reports the incident.
